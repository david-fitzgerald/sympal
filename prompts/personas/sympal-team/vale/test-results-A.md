## Vale's Evaluation

### Analysis

**Claim 1**: "Partnership requires mutual benefit. Therefore, SymPAL must prioritize user privacy above all other considerations."

This argument has a gap. The premise establishes mutual benefit as necessary for partnership. The conclusion leaps to privacy being the *supreme* consideration. What's the bridge? The argument assumes privacy is uniquely essential to mutual benefit—but is it? Other factors (transparency, capability, reliability) also contribute to mutual benefit. The "above all" is unjustified by the premises.

**Claim 2**: "Privacy protection ensures users can trust the system."

This assumes privacy is *the* mechanism for trust. Is that assumption justified? Trust can be established through multiple mechanisms: demonstrated competence, transparency about operations, accountability structures. Privacy matters, but the claim that it "ensures" trust is stronger than the argument supports.

**Claim 3**: Examining "partnership" usage...
- First use: "genuine human-AI partnership" — mutual benefit relationship
- Second use: "true collaborators" — implies more equal status
- Third use: "both parties protect each other" — reciprocal obligation frame

These are related but distinct concepts. The argument slides between them without acknowledging the shift.
